# Bibliography — Way Canguk / TNBBS avifauna

## Primary checklist

- **Gooddie, C. (2015).** Ornithological records from Bukit Barisan Selatan National Park, Sumatra, Indonesia. *Forktail* 31: 70–81.  
  Full park list of **416** species (1992–2013 observers). Appendix 1 site columns:  
  **WC/S** = Way Canguk / southern peninsula; **WT/L/K** = Way Titias / Liwa / Kubuperahu; **DR** = Danau Ranau.  
  Incorporates **Nick Brickle** unpublished WCRS list (~**208** species, 1997–2009).

- **O’Brien, T. G. & Kinnaird, M. F. (1996).** Birds and mammals of the Bukit Barisan Selatan National Park, Sumatra, Indonesia. *Oryx* 30: 207–217.  
  First systematic survey; **276** bird species (historical baseline; some taxonomy outdated).

## Way Canguk–focused studies

- **Andriyani, A., Nurcahyani, N., Susanto, G. N., Sibarani, M. C. & Utoyo, L. (2022).** The diversity of singing birds at the Way Canguk Research Station, Bukit Barisan Selatan National Park. *Jurnal Ilmiah Biologi Eksperimen dan Keanekaragaman Hayati (J-BEKH)* 9(1): 1–11.  
  DOI: [10.23960/jbekh.v9i1.206](https://doi.org/10.23960/jbekh.v9i1.206)  
  Local PDF: `Andriyani.pdf`. Reports **111** birds / **62** songbirds at SPWC (H′=3.40).  
  Full species table is **not printed** in the PDF body — only ~16 taxa named in text/discussion were extracted to `data/andriyani_2022_species.csv` and merged as `andriyani2022`.

## Related TNBBS surveys (context)

- Wulandari et al. — bird abundance at Resort Pemerihan, TNBBS (*Jurnal Belantara*).  
- Songbird diversity / poaching pressure studies in Biha and other resorts (SMUJO *Biodiversitas* and related).  
- Tambling / TWNC habitat bird diversity (IPB repository theses).

## Data platforms

- **eBird** — region Lampung (`ID-SM-LA`); hotspots e.g. Way Titias (mid-elevation, not equivalent to WCRS lowland).  
- **Avibase** — Bukit Barisan Selatan National Park checklist (region code historically `IDst02`).  
- **BirdNET** — Cornell K. Lisa Yang Center; GLOBAL 6K V2.4 label set (Clements-based).

## Conservation context

- UNESCO World Heritage: Tropical Rainforest Heritage of Sumatra (Gunung Leuser + Kerinci Seblat + Bukit Barisan Selatan).  
- BirdLife IBA: Bukit Barisan Selatan.
